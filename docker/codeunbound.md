A mix of voices is actually a secret weapon for engagement, often referred to in marketing as the **"Voice Numerosity Effect."**

* **Voice A (You):** Deep dives, complex troubleshooting, and "architect" level content.

### 1. Environment & Technical Setup

* **The "Closet Rule":** Record in a room with soft surfaces (carpets, curtains, or even a walk-in closet). Avoid kitchens or rooms with bare walls to prevent the "echo" that kills professional tech videos.
* **The "Two-Fist" Distance:** Keep the microphone about 6-8 inches (two fists) from your mouth.
* **Format:** Export as **WAV (48kHz, 24-bit)**. Avoid MP3 if possible, as we need the highest quality for post-processing.
* **The "Tail":** Leave **5 seconds of silence** at the start of every recording. This allows me to "sample" the room noise and remove it from the entire track.

### 2. Pacing & Tone (The "Helpful Peer")

* **The Tone:** Aim for **"Confident but Approachable."** Think of yourself as a helpful colleague explaining a solution, not a news anchor reading a script.
* **The "Comma Breath":** In tech, terminology is dense (e.g., *"Hyperland on Proxmox with iGPU passthrough"*). Pause for a half-second after complex technical terms to let the listener's brain catch up.
* **The Smile:** Literally smile while reading the intro and outro. It changes the "brightness" of the vocal cords and makes the video feel more welcoming.

### 3. Pronunciation Guide (The Tech "Dictionary")

*Tech terms are the biggest hurdle. Before recording, verify these common Arch/Proxmox terms:*

* **Arch:** "Arch" (like a doorway), not "Archie."
* **GUI:** "Goo-ey."
* **Sudo:** "Sue-doe."
* **Daemon:** "Dee-mon."
* **VNC:** (Read the letters) V-N-C.
* **Hyprland:** "Hyper-land."

### 4. Error Handling

* **The "Three-Clap" Rule:** If you stumble or mispronounce a word, **stop.** Clap your hands three times (this creates a visual spike in the editing software), wait 2 seconds, and restart the sentence.
* **Variations:** For the "Hook" (the first 30 seconds), provide **two versions**: one high-energy and one calm. This gives me options during the edit.
